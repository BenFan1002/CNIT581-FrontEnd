Problem Context

A small bookstore, "PageTurners", is looking to analyze its sales performance and customer engagement over the past year. The bookstore has been using a basic database to keep track of its inventory, sales, and customer information. With an expanding inventory and a growing customer base, PageTurners wants to understand which books are bestsellers, identify their most valuable customers, and optimize their stock levels.

Problem Description

The management team at PageTurners needs a series of SQL queries to extract insights from their data. The queries will help them make informed decisions about inventory purchases, marketing strategies, and customer loyalty programs.

  1. Bestselling Books: Identify the top 5 bestselling books, including the book title, author, and the number of copies sold.
  2. Valuable Customers: Determine the top 10 customers based on the total amount spent in the bookstore, including customer name and total expenditure.
  3. Stock Optimization: List all books that have been sold out (stock level equals zero) but are still in high demand (have been sold at least once in the past month).
  4. Customer Genre Preferences: For each customer, identify their most frequently purchased book genre.
  5. Monthly Sales Trend: Calculate the month-over-month percentage change in total sales volume to identify trends.
